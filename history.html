Wooden Hand Clubs

Before Europeans and European weapons arrived in Aotearoa, M훮ori warfare mainly involved hand-to-hand combat. Warriors used many different hand weapons, including taiaha (fighting staffs), pouwhenua (pointed staffs), tewhatewha (axe-like staffs), tao (short spears), huata (long spears) and patu (clubs). The long weapons were carried, and the small clubs were often tucked into a belt. Special weapons were sometimes given names and passed down through generations. At the National Weaving school, students are taught the skills and traditions of a craft hundreds of years old.

Cutlural Clothing

The image to the right is of a piupiu. Piupiu are cultural clothing significant to the Maori. They've been around pre-date European contact and have been estimated to date back to early 1900's. To this day they are still very significant and are used for ceremonies such as kapa haka. Piupiu are made from New Zealand flax and are processed by an elaborate method to create piupiu. This includes stripping the flax, twisted by a spinning method, boiled and then finally dried.
